# Introduction

**Herbst** is the German word for *autumn* — and this library was created to bring the best season of the year in Germany directly into your project: the warm, sepia light of a fall in the heart of Europe, accompanied by a single ember glowing quietly.

Copy-and-paste Angular components. More than sixty-five customizable elements for your project, each one carrying that season into your interface — leaves falling over cobblestone streets, trees turned amber and rust, chestnut groves and the quiet mist of a German October morning. Building with Herbst UI should evoke the feeling of a clear German October: calm, welcoming, and unmistakably its own.

## Philosophy

- **The code is your project's.** Components live in your repository, not behind a package boundary — fully customizable. Read them, adapt them, delete what you don't need.
- **One source of truth for style.** Every component reads semantic tokens (`bg-primary`, `text-muted-foreground`, `border-border`). Edit one theme file and the whole system re-themes.
- **Signals-first Angular.** Standalone components, `input()`/`model()`/`output()`, `OnPush` throughout. No modules.
- **A real identity.** A look of its own, not a generic template — quiet, warm and unmistakable.

## What's inside

The catalogue spans the whole surface of an application — overlays, forms, data display, navigation, feedback and layout. Buttons and inputs, dialogs and drawers, tables and trees, a command palette, charts, a calendar, and more.

Browse them all in the [Components](/en/docs/components) catalogue.
